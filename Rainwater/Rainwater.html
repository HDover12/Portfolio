Rainwater Challenge

The Challenge

For this challenge, I created a capturingRainwater() function that takes in an array of heights, and returns the amount of rainwater that could be contained in that array. For example, the array [4, 2, 1, 3, 0, 1, 2] would give the folowing histogram:



This means that a total of 6 units of water could be captured in this example.

My Solution

My approach to this challenge was to loop through each number in the array and slice out the section before that number and the section after that number. I then found the maxium number before and after. Water would only collect up to the second highest of those two maximum numbers. I could then subtract the second highest number from the height of the current number and that would give me how much water that spot could hold. All that would be left would be to add up each spot.
